My career has developed across quality control, process control, process engineering, and production management. Across each role, the objective has remained consistent: build manufacturing systems that perform reliably under real operating conditions.

I approach performance gaps as system problems rather than isolated incidents. The process begins by understanding actual operating conditions, identifying constraints, quantifying losses, and aligning the relevant functions around root causes. Improvements are then converted into practical standards, operating controls, ownership routines, and performance reviews. The objective is not only to solve the current issue. It is to establish a stronger operating system that continues to perform after the improvement project is completed.
